**Author:** Daniel Kahneman  
**Type:** Book  
**Rating:** ★★★★★

## Summary

Kahneman introduces a framework of two cognitive systems: System 1 operates automatically and quickly, while System 2 allocates attention to effortful mental activities. The interplay between these two systems explains many of the biases and heuristics that affect human judgment.

## What I Found Interesting

The chapters on cognitive biases — anchoring, availability heuristic, and loss aversion — are particularly relevant to how we design and evaluate data systems. The idea that humans are poor intuitive statisticians resonates strongly with challenges I have seen in data-driven decision-making.

## Key Takeaways

- Humans rely heavily on System 1, leading to systematic errors in judgment.
- Awareness of biases does not automatically correct them.
- Statistical thinking is a skill that requires deliberate effort.
